Focus3 — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-04-27

Focus3 is a personal productivity app. This page describes what data Focus3 stores, how it's processed, and what you can do with it.

What we store

  • Tasks, goals, and preferences you create in the app. In the local / single-user version these live only in your browser's localStorage. In the multi-user version they live in our Supabase Postgres database, scoped to your account by row-level security.
  • Google Calendar OAuth tokens (when you connect a calendar) — stored locally as .google-tokens.json in single-user mode, or in the google_tokens table keyed by your user id in multi-user mode. Used solely to read events and create / delete events on the primary calendar of the account you connected.
  • AI ranking cache — Claude's last tier + reasoning per task, kept in your browser's localStorage so we don't re-call the model on every reload.

What we send to third parties

  • Anthropic (Claude): when you click Refresh AI, Brain dump, Suggest dates, or Scan back, we send the relevant task text (title, description prefix up to 200 chars, urgency, due date, theme, recurrence) and / or the planner image. We do not send full personal histories or unrelated tasks.
  • Google Calendar API: read events from calendars you've selected; create / delete events you explicitly schedule via Focus3. No background sync.
  • Companies House (UK): when you add a task that mentions a UK company name, we look the company up via the public Companies House search API to fetch filing dates. The lookup is triggered by the task title text only.

What you can do

  • Export everything — Settings → Backup & restore → Export backup. You get a JSON file with every task, goal, and pref.
  • Delete everything — open Settings, click Disconnect Calendar (clears Google tokens), and clear your browser data / delete your Supabase account to remove the rest.
  • Disconnect Google at any time — Settings → Disconnect Calendar revokes Focus3's access locally. To revoke OAuth at Google's side too, visit myaccount.google.com/permissions.

Cookies

Focus3 doesn't use tracking cookies. Multi-user mode uses a Supabase auth session cookie strictly to keep you signed in.
